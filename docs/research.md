# Research Base

Community Check is grounded in peer-reviewed research on misperception, social norms, and platform dynamics. These are the key findings that motivate and support the design.

## Core Findings

### The Perception Gap

- **Lee, Neumann, Zaki & Hancock (2025).** "Americans overestimate how many social media users post harmful content." *PNAS Nexus*, 4(12). — 3.1% of users post toxic content; Americans estimate 43% (13x overestimate). Correcting this overestimate improved positive emotions and reduced perceived moral decline. [doi.org/10.1093/pnasnexus/pgaf310](https://doi.org/10.1093/pnasnexus/pgaf310)

- **Ahler & Sood (2018).** "The Parties in Our Heads: Misperceptions about Party Composition and Their Consequences." *Journal of Politics*, 80(3). — Americans overestimate party-stereotypical group membership by 342%. Democrats overestimate the share of Republicans who are wealthy; Republicans overestimate the share of Democrats who are atheists. [doi.org/10.1086/697253](https://doi.org/10.1086/697253)

- **Moore-Berg, Ankori-Karlinsky, Hameiri & Bruneau (2020).** "Exaggerated meta-perceptions predict intergroup hostility between American political partisans." *PNAS*, 117(26). — Partisans overestimate the other side's hostility by roughly 2x. The more inaccurate the perception, the stronger the dehumanization. [doi.org/10.1073/pnas.2001263117](https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.2001263117)

- **Sparkman, Geiger & Weber (2022).** "Americans experience a false social reality by underestimating popular climate policy support by nearly half." *Nature Communications*, 13, 4779. — 80% of Americans support renewable energy siting, but people estimate 43%. [doi.org/10.1038/s41467-022-32412-y](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-022-32412-y)

- **Yudkin, Hawkins & Dixon (2019).** "The Perception Gap." More in Common. — The most politically engaged Americans have perception gaps nearly 3x larger than disengaged Americans. Media consumption and political engagement worsen accuracy. [perceptiongap.us](https://perceptiongap.us/)

### Corrections Work

- **Mernyk, Pink, Druckman & Willer (2022).** "Correcting Inaccurate Metaperceptions Reduces Americans' Support for Partisan Violence." *PNAS*, 119(16). — A single correction of inaccurate beliefs about partisan hostility reduced support for partisan violence, with effects lasting ~26 days. n=4,741. [doi.org/10.1073/pnas.2116851119](https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.2116851119)

### The Spiral of Silence

- **Noelle-Neumann (1974).** "The Spiral of Silence: A Theory of Public Opinion." *Journal of Communication*, 24(2). — When people believe they hold a minority view, they self-censor. This creates a feedback loop where the perceived majority grows louder and the actual majority goes quiet. [doi.org/10.1111/j.1460-2466.1974.tb00367.x](https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1460-2466.1974.tb00367.x)

- **Hampton, Rainie, Lu, Shin & Purcell (2014).** "Social Media and the 'Spiral of Silence'." Pew Research Center. — Social media amplifies the spiral: people who sensed their Facebook network disagreed with them were less likely to speak up both online and in person. [pewresearch.org](https://www.pewresearch.org/internet/2014/08/26/social-media-and-the-spiral-of-silence/)

- **Oz, Shahin & Greeves (2024).** "Platform affordances and spiral of silence: How perceived differences between Facebook and Twitter influence opinion expression online." *Technology in Society*, 76. — Contemporary empirical confirmation that the spiral-of-silence dynamic operates on social media. Fear of social isolation suppresses opinion expression, with platform-specific affordances (network association, anonymity, social presence) moderating the effect. [doi.org/10.1016/j.techsoc.2023.102431](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.techsoc.2023.102431)

### Minority Dominance on Platforms

- **Grinberg, Joseph, Friedland, Swire-Thompson & Lazer (2019).** "Fake news on Twitter during the 2016 U.S. presidential election." *Science*, 363(6425). — 0.1% of users shared 80% of fake news during the 2016 election. [doi.org/10.1126/science.aau2706](https://doi.org/10.1126/science.aau2706)

- **Baribi-Bartov, Munger & Pan (2024).** "Supersharers of fake news on Twitter." *Science*, 384(6700). — A tiny fraction of accounts are responsible for the vast majority of misinformation sharing. [doi.org/10.1126/science.adl4435](https://doi.org/10.1126/science.adl4435)

- **Bail (2021).** *Breaking the Social Media Prism.* Princeton University Press. — The most politically extreme users dominate online discourse, creating a distorted mirror of public opinion. [Princeton University Press](https://press.princeton.edu/books/hardcover/9780691203423/breaking-the-social-media-prism)

### Social Norms Research

- **Wojcieszak (2008).** "False Consensus Goes Online: An Analysis of the Relative Frequency Hypothesis." *Public Opinion Quarterly*, 72(4). — Online environments amplify false consensus effects. [doi.org/10.1093/poq/nfn056](https://doi.org/10.1093/poq/nfn056)

- **Pinker (2025).** *When Everyone Knows That Everyone Knows.* Scribner. — Common knowledge (what we know that everyone knows) shapes social norms and collective behavior. Making hidden consensus visible changes what's possible. [Simon & Schuster](https://www.simonandschuster.com/books/When-Everyone-Knows-That-Everyone-Knows/Steven-Pinker/9781668011577)

## Related Systems

- **Community Notes (X/Twitter):** Evaluates factual accuracy of posts via bridging-based volunteer consensus. [Wojcik et al., 2022](https://arxiv.org/abs/2210.15723). Complementary to Community Check — Notes assesses truth; Community Check shows opinion distribution.
